The Solution

A platform &
a merchant network

  • Shariah-compliant BNPL — powered by an NBFC balance-sheet partner
  • Merchant network — consumers discover partners, merchants get leads
  • One product. Two revenue streams. Built for the unbanked majority
  • Entry via SECP Regulatory Sandbox — no upfront capital required
Transaction Flow

Simple at the
point of sale

01
Consumer requests BNPL at the merchant
02
CNIC + phone number. Decision returned in under 5 minutes
03
Consumer picks repayment — 3 to 6 months, chosen freely
04
Consumer signs a Shariah-compliant digital agreement
05
NBFC partner pays the merchant. Consumer repays on schedule.

Revenue Model

Three streams.
Two sides.

Merchant Side

Commission + Subscription
  • Commission per transaction — rate TBD by vertical
  • Monthly SaaS for platform access, dashboard, and lead visibility
  • Sold on BNPL. Retained by lead generation.

Consumer Side

Optional Membership
  • Free tier is genuinely useful — no artificial forcing
  • Paid tier earns its fee via the no-upfront mechanic
  • Pricing TBD — validated on the ground
Risk Management

Deterrence over
enforcement

Primary
Credit Bureau Reporting
Every transaction and default reported. SECP-mandated. Powerful for anyone who wants future credit access.
High Tickets
Guarantor Model
Co-signer required above a set threshold. Works naturally within Pakistan's family culture.
Network
Blacklisting
Defaulters lose access to the entire merchant network. More powerful as the network grows.
Regulatory Path

Sandbox first.
License later.

01
SECP Regulatory Sandbox — no minimum capital to apply. 6–12 months supervised live operation.
02
NBFC balance-sheet partner via existing banking contacts. They carry the credit risk.
03
Live repayment data used to raise capital and pursue full NBFC licensing post-sandbox.
